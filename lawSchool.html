Law School with Kids

I started law school with a 2 year old and 3 month old baby. My husband had just finished his 2nd year of medical school and was beginning the Phd part of his education. It was a ludicrous scheme. We were new, even clownish parents, and we didn't have a family network of support in the state. But we moved forward with faith, that somehow it would all work out. Pitt Law allowed me to do a "Flex-time" schedule. That meant I crammed all my 1L classes from 8 to 12. I did torts with one section, criminal law with another, etc. It was hard and allowed no dedicated study time, but it was a way forward.

Would I do it that way if I could do it again? Absolutely NOT! I would find a good babysitter and take out the necessary loans to pay for one. I'd ask my husband to share more of the childcare. I'd ask family to fly in during finals. I'd prepare the entire summer before law school.

Having said that, I've had to reconcile with myself -- my husband and I were young parents in our 20's. We tried, even doing the best that we could do. We didn't understand things as well as we do now. In the end, we ended up with degrees. We learned from our mistakes.
