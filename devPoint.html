DevPoint Labs Bootcamp

In January of 2021, I enrolled in a simple IT class at the local community college, Davis Tech. It provided a good overview, and I took the CompTIA(ITF+)certification at the end of it. After this I asked one of the teachers if I could skip through the hardware stuff and take a programming class and they let me take the first programming basics class, which was taught with Visual Studio; we learned Visual Basic.

I learned a lot with that first coding class, but I could tell that it was outdated. I talked to the guys over at Dev Point Labs, which do a 3 month coding boot camp. I opted for the part-time program which held class from 6-9 Mon-Thurs. I ultimately learned ruby as the backend; javascript as the frontend. Although, we learned gradually to some degree, using ruby on rails at first, with only a bit of javascript.

What did I learn?

I learned that I have a lot to learn about coding. This bootcamp was a good intro, but that I in no way was prepared to enter the workforce as any type of coder.

Were other students prepared?

Maybe. Probably. I was one of 5 in my cohort. We all came from different backgrounds - an English professor, me (a non-practicing attorney), a computer science student with a couple of years of relevant schooling, a new computer science student that had just changed majors, and a Costco employee. The computer science student that was well into his program was the most confident after the program.

Was the instruction lacking?

Not at all! I just think that 3 months in a part-time program is not enough time for most people of normal intelligence to absorb the material!
