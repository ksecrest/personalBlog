DevPoint Labs Bootcamp

In January of 2021, I enrolled in a simple IT class at the local community college, Davis Tech. It provided a good overview, and I took the CompTIA(ITF+)certification at the end of it. After this I asked one of the teachers if I could skip through the hardware stuff and take a programming class and they let me take the first programming basics class, which was taught with Visual Studio; we learned Visual Basic.

I learned a lot with that first coding class, but I could tell that it was getting dated. I talked to the guys over at Dev Point Labs, which do a 3 month coding boot camp. I opted for the part-time program which held class from 6-9 Mon-Thurs. I ultimately learned ruby as the backend; javascript as the frontend. Although, we learned gradually to some degree, using ruby on rails at first, with only a bit of javascript.

What did I learn?

I learned that I have a lot to learn about coding. This bootcamp was a good intro, but that I wanted to learn more before I was confident to enter the workforce as any type of coder. DevPoint does an awesome job of getting a lot taught in a short amount of time.

Were other students more prepared?

Probably. I was one of only 5 in my cohort. We all came from different backgrounds - an English professor, me (a non-practicing attorney), a computer science student with a couple of years of relevant schooling, a new computer science student that had just changed majors, and another student. The computer science student that was well into his program was the most confident after the program.

Was the instruction lacking?

Not at all! Just a lot of material for the typical person.
