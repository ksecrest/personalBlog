Out of laziness, I often call myself a vegan. But, I actually try to follow a whole food plant-based diet. The fact that I “try” means that I still have progress to make, probably always will, but I’m really not a vegan in the traditional sense. Veganism used to be diet concerned with avoiding dairy, beef, poultry, fish, pork, or fish to avoid cruelty to animals, but now it has evolved to include those that do not use animal-based products for whatever reason. Vegans generally do not use animal products like honey or gelatin or wear or buy items made out of leather or other animal skins.

Truly, my conscience is more at peace these days about animals in general. However, avoiding animal cruelty was not the reason I stopped consuming meat or dairy. It had completely to do with health and suddenly everything making sense. Simply, I had thought for years in my heart, that the right way to eat probably do not include meat or dairy, and yet I didn’t know why. Then I stumbled upon a few of the right books, and I learned about all the research that supports NOT eating meat or dairy WHILE eating lots of whole fruits, vegetables, and grains. It aligned with the Word of Wisdom, which is a section within a religious text of my faith.
